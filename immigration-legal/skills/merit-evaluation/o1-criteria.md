# O-1 Criteria Reference
## 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3) — Extraordinary Ability or Achievement

*Source: 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(ii)–(iii). Cross-reference: USCIS Policy Manual, Vol. 2, Part M, Ch. 4.*

---

## Two O-1 classifications

**O-1A:** Sciences, Education, Business, or Athletics — extraordinary *ability* in the field.
**O-1B:** Arts, Motion Picture, or Television — extraordinary *achievement* in the field.

The criteria differ between O-1A and O-1B. Both are addressed below.

---

## O-1A — Sciences, Education, Business, or Athletics

### Standard

The beneficiary must be at the top of their field — defined by widespread acclaim and international recognition. O-1A shares the "extraordinary ability" standard with EB-1A but is a nonimmigrant classification filed by a petitioner/employer on the beneficiary's behalf (or by an agent).

### Evidentiary criteria (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(ii))

The beneficiary must have received a major internationally recognized award (e.g., a Nobel Prize), OR meet at least **3 of the following 8 criteria**:

#### Criterion O1A-1 — Prizes or Awards
Receipt of nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence in the field of endeavor.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 1.*

#### Criterion O1A-2 — Membership in Associations Requiring Outstanding Achievement
Membership in associations that require outstanding achievements, judged by recognized national or international experts.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 2.*

#### Criterion O1A-3 — Published Material About the Beneficiary
Published material in professional or major trade publications or other major media about the beneficiary, relating to their work.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 3.*

#### Criterion O1A-4 — Judging the Work of Others
Participation as a judge of the work of others in the same or an allied field.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 4.*

#### Criterion O1A-5 — Original Scientific, Scholarly, or Business-Related Contributions
Original scientific, scholarly, or business-related contributions of major significance in the field.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 5.*

#### Criterion O1A-6 — Authorship of Scholarly Articles
Authorship of scholarly articles in professional journals or other major media.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 6.*

#### Criterion O1A-7 — Critical or Essential Capacity for Distinguished Organizations
Evidence that the beneficiary has been employed in a critical or essential capacity for organizations and establishments that have a distinguished reputation.

*Note: This criterion combines elements of EB-1A Criteria 8 and — to a lesser extent — 9. "Critical or essential capacity" is comparable to "leading or critical role" in EB-1A.*

**Key interpretive points specific to O-1A:**
- The O-1 context makes this criterion particularly important for startup founders and early employees — a critical technical or executive role at a distinguished startup (distinguished by funding, user base, or press recognition) can satisfy this.
- "Distinguished reputation" of the organization does not require a Fortune 500 company. Evidence of funding rounds, industry recognition, or press coverage establishing the organization's reputation in its field suffices.

#### Criterion O1A-8 — High Salary or Remuneration
Evidence that the beneficiary has commanded a high salary or other high remuneration for services.

*Same interpretive framework as EB-1A Criterion 9.*

### Comparable evidence (O-1A)

If the above criteria do not readily apply to the beneficiary's occupation (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(ii)), the petitioner may submit comparable evidence of extraordinary ability. The petitioner must first establish that the listed criteria do not readily apply, then submit evidence that is genuinely comparable.

*Firm posture on comparable evidence: see `## Evidence standards` → "O-1 comparable evidence" in the practice profile.*

## O-1B — Arts, Motion Picture, or Television

### Standard — Arts

O-1B (Arts) requires distinction — defined as a high level of achievement in the arts evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition substantially above that ordinarily encountered. "Arts" is broadly defined to include fine arts, visual arts, culinary arts, and performing arts.

### Standard — Motion Picture or Television

O-1B (MPTV) requires a record of extraordinary achievement evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition significantly above that normally encountered in the field.

*Note: The O-1B (MPTV) standard is lower than O-1B (Arts) — "extraordinary achievement" for MPTV vs. "distinction" for arts. Both are lower than O-1A's "extraordinary ability."*

### Evidentiary criteria — O-1B (Arts) (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(A))

To meet the "distinction" standard for O-1B (Arts), the beneficiary must meet at least **3 of the following 6 criteria** OR have performed or will perform services as a lead or starring participant in distinguished productions:

#### Criterion O1B-A1 — Lead or Starring Role in Distinguished Production
Evidence that the alien has performed, and will perform, services as a lead or starring participant in productions or events which have a distinguished reputation as evidenced by critical reviews, advertisements, publicity releases, publications, contracts, or endorsements.

#### Criterion O1B-A2 — Critical Role in Distinguished Organization
Evidence that the alien has achieved national or international recognition for achievements evidenced by critical reviews or other published materials by or about the individual in major newspapers, trade journals, magazines, or other publications.

#### Criterion O1B-A3 — Record of Major Commercial or Critically Acclaimed Success
Evidence that the alien has performed, and will perform, in a lead, starring, or critical role for organizations and establishments that have a distinguished reputation evidenced by articles in newspapers, trade journals, publications, or testimonials.

#### Criterion O1B-A4 — Significant Recognition from Organizations, Critics, Government Agencies, or Experts
A record of major commercial or critically acclaimed successes as evidenced by such indicators as title, rating, standing in the field, box office receipts, motion pictures or television ratings, and other occupational achievements reported in trade journals, major newspapers, or other publications.

#### Criterion O1B-A5 — High Salary
Evidence that the alien has received significant recognition for achievements from organizations, critics, government agencies, or other recognized experts in the field in which the alien is engaged. Such testimonials must be in a form which clearly indicates the author's authority, expertise, and knowledge of the alien's achievements.

#### Criterion O1B-A6 — Other Comparable Evidence
Evidence that the alien commands a high salary or other substantial remuneration for services evidenced by contracts or other reliable evidence.

### Evidentiary criteria — O-1B (MPTV) (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(B))

## O-1 vs. EB-1A: key differences

| Factor | O-1A | EB-1A |
|---|---|---|
| Classification | Nonimmigrant (temporary) | Immigrant (permanent) |
| Petition filed by | Employer or agent | Employer or self |
| Standard | Extraordinary ability | Extraordinary ability (same standard, higher scrutiny in practice) |
| Criterion threshold | 3 of 8 (O-1A) | 3 of 10 |
| Processing | Regular + Premium (15 days) | Regular + Premium |
| Advisory opinion | Required from peer group / union | Not required |
| Key distinction | More flexible for short-track; no USCIS national interest test | Leads to permanent residence |

In practice, an O-1A approval is often used to support a subsequent EB-1A petition but is not a guarantee of EB-1A approval. Evidence standards and adjudicator scrutiny differ.

## Advisory opinion requirement

O-1 petitions require a written advisory opinion from a peer group, labor organization, or person with expertise in the field. The petitioner must submit:
- A written advisory opinion from the appropriate union or guild (if one exists), OR
- A written advisory opinion from a person with expertise in the field (if no appropriate union/guild exists), OR
- Evidence that no appropriate union or guild exists + expert opinion

The advisory opinion must attest to the beneficiary's extraordinary ability/achievement and to the nature of the duties to be performed.

*Note for attorneys: advisory opinions are from peer organizations, not from the beneficiary's employer. They are distinct from expert support letters.*

---

## O-1 consultation waiver

USCIS may waive the consultation requirement for O-1A petitions if the beneficiary has received prior O-1A approval within the past two years and there is no material change. Document the prior approval and request the waiver explicitly.
