# Leave and Conditions — BCEA Leave Framework

South African overlay for leave-tracking and wage-hour skills. Covers all statutory leave types under the BCEA, working time provisions, notice periods, and deduction rules.

---

## Annual leave (BCEA s20)

### Entitlement

An employee is entitled to **21 consecutive days** of annual leave on full remuneration in respect of each annual leave cycle (BCEA s20(2)(a)).

By **written agreement**, the entitlement may be calculated as **one day of leave for every 17 days worked** (BCEA s20(2)(b)). This formula accommodates irregular work patterns.

### Key rules

| Rule | Detail | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Annual leave cycle | 12 months from date of employment or completion of prior cycle | BCEA s20(1) |
| Grant timing | Leave must be granted within 6 months after end of cycle | BCEA s20(3) |
| No cashing out during employment | Annual leave may not be paid in lieu during the employment relationship — the employee must take the leave | BCEA s20(6) |
| Payout on termination | Accrued but untaken annual leave must be paid out on termination | BCEA s20(5) |
| Public holidays during leave | Public holidays that fall during annual leave do not count as leave days | BCEA s20(9) |
| Sick leave during annual leave | If an employee falls ill during annual leave, the employer may agree to convert those days to sick leave (at the employer's discretion) | Best practice, not statutory mandate |

### Above-minimum provisions

The BCEA sets a **floor**. Employers may (and commonly do) provide more generous annual leave. Where the employment contract or a bargaining council agreement provides for more than 21 days, the more favourable term applies.

## Sick leave (BCEA s22)

### Entitlement

An employee is entitled to paid sick leave equal to the number of days the employee would ordinarily work during a period of **six weeks** in every **36-month cycle** of employment (BCEA s22(2)).

For a standard 5-day-week employee, this equates to **30 working days** per 36-month sick leave cycle.

### First six months

During the first six months of employment, an employee is entitled to **one day of paid sick leave for every 26 days worked** (BCEA s22(3)). The full entitlement is available after 6 months.

### Medical certificate requirements

| Absence duration | Medical certificate required? | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 consecutive days | Not required unless the employee has been absent more than twice in an 8-week period | BCEA s23(1) |
| More than 2 consecutive days | Required | BCEA s23(1) |
| Pattern of short absences (more than 2 in 8 weeks) | Certificate may be required for every absence | BCEA s23(1) |

The medical certificate must be issued by a **medical practitioner, traditional health practitioner, or any other person who is certified to diagnose and treat patients** and who is registered with a professional council established by an Act of Parliament (BCEA s23(2)).

### Exhaustion of sick leave

Once the 36-month cycle entitlement is exhausted, the employer is not obliged to pay for further sick leave. The employee may:

- Take unpaid leave.
- Use annual leave (by agreement).
- Apply for illness benefits through the **Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF)** if the incapacity is expected to last more than 14 days.

If the illness constitutes incapacity, the employer must follow the incapacity procedure under **LRA Schedule 8 Items 10-11** before considering dismissal.

## Family responsibility leave (BCEA s27)

### Entitlement

An employee is entitled to **3 days** of paid family responsibility leave per annual leave cycle (BCEA s27(2)).

### Eligibility

The employee must:

- Have been employed for **longer than 4 months** (BCEA s27(1)(a)).
- Work at least **4 days per week** for the employer (BCEA s27(1)(b)).

### Qualifying events

Family responsibility leave may be taken when:

- The employee's **child is born** (BCEA s27(2)(a)).
- The employee's **child is sick** (BCEA s27(2)(a)).
- In the event of the **death** of the employee's spouse or life partner, parent, adoptive parent, grandparent, child, adopted child, grandchild, or sibling (BCEA s27(2)(b)).

### Documentation

The employer may require reasonable proof of the event (BCEA s27(3)).

### Unused leave

Family responsibility leave that is not taken during the annual leave cycle **lapses** at the end of the cycle — it does not accumulate (BCEA s27(4)).

## Maternity leave (BCEA s25)

### Entitlement

An employee is entitled to at least **4 consecutive months** of maternity leave (BCEA s25(1)).

### Timing

- The employee may commence maternity leave at any time from **4 weeks before the expected date of birth**, or on an earlier date if a medical practitioner or midwife certifies that it is necessary (BCEA s25(2)).
- An employee may **not work for 6 weeks after the birth** of her child, unless a medical practitioner or midwife certifies that she is fit to do so (BCEA s25(3)).

### Remuneration

Maternity leave under the BCEA is **unpaid**. However, the employee may claim maternity benefits from the **Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF)** under the Unemployment Insurance Act 63 of 2001. UIF maternity benefits are paid at a sliding scale based on the employee's remuneration, up to a capped maximum.

Many employers voluntarily supplement UIF benefits with partial or full pay during maternity leave. Any such arrangement should be clearly recorded in the employment contract or a maternity leave policy.

### Miscarriage and stillbirth

An employee who has a **miscarriage in the third trimester** of pregnancy or who bears a **stillborn child** is entitled to maternity leave for **6 weeks** after the miscarriage or stillbirth, whether or not the employee had commenced maternity leave at the time (BCEA s25(4)).

### No dismissal during maternity

Dismissal of an employee on grounds of pregnancy is **automatically unfair** under LRA s187(1)(e). This protection extends to the period of maternity leave.

## Paternity and parental leave

### Labour Laws Amendment Act 10 of 2018

The Labour Laws Amendment Act 10 of 2018 introduced:

- **Parental leave:** An employee who is a parent of a child is entitled to **10 consecutive days** of parental leave, commencing on the date of birth of the child or the date the adoption order is granted (s25A of the BCEA as inserted by the Amendment Act).
- **Adoption leave:** One adopting parent is entitled to **10 consecutive weeks** of adoption leave. Where there are two adopting parents, one may take adoption leave and the other may take parental leave of 10 days (s25B).
- **Commissioning parental leave:** A commissioning parent in a surrogate motherhood agreement is entitled to **10 consecutive weeks** of commissioning parental leave (s25C).

### Remuneration

Parental leave (10 days) is **unpaid**, but the employee may claim benefits from the **UIF**. Adoption leave and commissioning parental leave (10 weeks) are also unpaid with UIF benefits available.

## Working time (BCEA ss9-16)

### Threshold applicability

Working time provisions in BCEA ss9-16 apply **only to employees earning below the BCEA earnings threshold** (currently R254,371.67 per annum). Employees earning above the threshold are excluded from these provisions, though employers may contractually agree to apply them.

### Ordinary hours

| Provision | Limit | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum ordinary hours per week | **45 hours** | BCEA s9(1) |
| Maximum ordinary hours per day (5-day week) | **9 hours** | BCEA s9(1)(a) |
| Maximum ordinary hours per day (6-day week) | **8 hours** | BCEA s9(1)(b) |
| Compressed working week (by agreement) | Up to **12 hours per day** without overtime, not exceeding 45 per week, no more than 5 days per week | BCEA s11 |

### Overtime

| Provision | Rule | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum overtime per week | **10 hours** | BCEA s10(1) |
| Overtime rate | **1.5x** normal wage | BCEA s10(2) |
| Agreement required | Overtime may only be worked by agreement (not compelled unilaterally) | BCEA s10(1) |
| Time off instead of overtime pay | By agreement, paid time off equal to the overtime worked may be granted | BCEA s10(4) |

### Sunday work

| Provision | Rate | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Employee who does not ordinarily work Sundays | **2x** normal wage | BCEA s16(1)(a) |
| Employee who ordinarily works Sundays | **1.5x** normal wage | BCEA s16(1)(b) |

### Night work (BCEA s17)

An employer may only require or permit an employee to perform night work (work between 18:00 and 06:00) if:

- The employee has agreed to do so.
- The employee is compensated by an allowance or by a reduction of working hours.
- Transportation is available between the employee's place of residence and the workplace at the commencement and conclusion of the shift.

### Meal interval (BCEA s14)

An employer must give an employee who works continuously for more than **5 hours** a meal interval of at least **60 minutes** (BCEA s14(1)). By written agreement, the meal interval may be reduced to **30 minutes** (BCEA s14(2)).

## Notice periods (BCEA s37)

### Minimum statutory notice

| Length of service | Minimum notice period | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| 6 months or less | **1 week** | BCEA s37(1)(a) |
| More than 6 months but not more than 1 year | **2 weeks** | BCEA s37(1)(b) |
| 1 year or more | **4 weeks** | BCEA s37(1)(c) |

### Key rules

- Notice must be given in **writing** (BCEA s37(2)).
- Notice may not be given **during any period of leave** to which the employee is entitled under the Act (BCEA s37(3)).
- A contract may provide for a **longer** notice period than the statutory minimum, but not a shorter one.
- For employees employed as **farm workers or domestic workers** who have been employed for more than 6 months, the minimum notice period is **4 weeks** (BCEA s37(1)(c) read with Sectoral Determination 7 and 13).
- Payment in lieu of notice is permissible by agreement or custom.

## Deductions from remuneration (BCEA s34)

### General rule

An employer may **not** make any deduction from an employee's remuneration unless the employee has agreed to the deduction in writing, and the deduction is for a purpose specified in the agreement (BCEA s34(1)).

### Deductions for damage or loss

A deduction for loss or damage caused by the employee in the course of employment is permissible only if:

- The loss or damage occurred in the course of employment (BCEA s34(2)(a)).
- The employer has followed a **fair procedure** and the employee has been given a reasonable opportunity to show why the deduction should not be made (BCEA s34(2)(b)).
- The total deduction does not exceed **the actual amount of the loss or damage** (BCEA s34(2)(b)).
- The deduction does not exceed **one-quarter (25%) of the employee's remuneration in money** per payment period (BCEA s34(2)(c)).

### Prohibited deductions

Deductions that are not permitted include:

- Deductions that reduce the employee's remuneration below the minimum wage (if a sectoral determination or bargaining council minimum applies).
- Fines (unless provided for in a collective agreement).
- Deductions without written consent.
